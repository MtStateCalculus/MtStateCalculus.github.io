\(f(125) = 70\) and \(f'(125) = 6\) tell us that at \(x = 125\text{,}\) the value of the function is 70 and the function is increasing at a rate of 6 units for a unit increase in \(x\text{.}\) Since we increase \(x\) by -2 units in going from 125 to 123, the value of the function goes up by approximately \(-2 \cdot 6 = -12\) units, so
\begin{equation*} f(123) \approx 70 - 2 \cdot 6 = 70 - 12 = 58. \end{equation*}
