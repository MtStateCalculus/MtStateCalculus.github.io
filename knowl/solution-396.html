Solution 6.4.5.4.1.

(a) We slice the water horizontally. Each slice is a cylindrical slab of radius and thickness \(\Delta h\text{,}\) so
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Volume of each slab }\approx \pi 2^2 \Delta h\hbox{ ft}^3. \end{equation*}
The density of water is \(\delta = 62.4\) lb/ft\({}^3\text{,}\) so
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Weight of slab }\approx \delta\pi2^2 \Delta h \hbox{ lb.} \end{equation*}
Water at a height of \(h\) ft must be lifted a distance of \(10 - h\) ft.
\begin{equation*} \hbox{ Work to move one slice} =\delta \cdot \hbox{Volume} \cdot \hbox{Distance lifted} \approx \delta (\pi (2)^2 \Delta h)(10 - h)\hbox{ ft-lb.} \end{equation*}
Plugging in \(\delta=62.4\) and since \(h\) varies from \(h = 0\) to \(h = 10\text{,}\) the total work, \(W\text{,}\) is:
\begin{equation*} W = \int_0^{10}{\delta(\pi 2^2 )(10-h)dh} = 2^2\delta \pi \int_0^{10}{(10 - h)dh} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = (2^2) 62.4 \pi\left(10 h-\frac{h^2}2\right) \Bigg|_0^{10} = 39207.1\ {\rm lbf}. \end{equation*}
The total work required is \(39207.1\ {\rm lbf}\text{.}\) (b) This is the same as part (a) except the water must be lifted a distance of \(17 - h\) ft. The total work is:
\begin{equation*} W = \int_0^{10}{\delta(\pi 2^2 )(7-h)dh} = 2^2\delta \pi \int_0^{10}{(17 - h)dh} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = (2^2) 62.4 \pi \left(17 h-\frac{h^2}2\right)\Bigg|_0^{10} = 94097\ {\rm lbf}. \end{equation*}
The total work required is \(94097\ {\rm lbf}\text{.}\) (c) This is the same as part (a) except that \(h\) varies from \(h=0\) to \(h=7\text{.}\) The total work is:
\begin{equation*} W = \int_0^{7}{\delta(\pi 2^2 )(10-h)dh} = 2^2\delta \pi \int_0^{7}{(10 -h)dh} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = (2^2) 62.4 \pi \left(10 h-\frac{h^2}2\right)\Bigg|_0^{10} = 35678.4\ {\rm lbf}. \end{equation*}
The total work required is \(35678.4\ {\rm lbf}\text{.}\)
