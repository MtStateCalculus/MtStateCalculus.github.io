Solution 8.6.4.1.1.

\(\displaystyle \frac{3}{1-3 x} = 3 \cdot \sum_{n=0}^\infty (3 x)^n\text{.}\) Then we can see that \(c_n = 3 \cdot 3^n\text{,}\) making \(c_0 = 3\text{,}\) \(c_1 = 3 \cdot 3 = 9\text{,}\) \(c_2 = 3 \cdot 3^2 = 27\text{,}\) etc. Regarding the radius of convergence, we perform the standard ratio test:
\begin{equation*} \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| \frac{3(3 x)^{n+1}}{3(3 x)^n} \right| = \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| 3 x \right| = \left| 3 x \right| \end{equation*}
And then \(\left| 3 x \right| \lt 1 \iff \left| x \right| \lt \frac 1 {3}\text{,}\) making \(R = \frac 1 {3}\text{.}\)
