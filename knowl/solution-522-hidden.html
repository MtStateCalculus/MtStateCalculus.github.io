\(\cos(x)\) and its first three derivatives are \(f(x) = \cos(x)\text{,}\) \(f'(x) = - \sin(x)\text{,}\) \(f''(x) = - \cos(x)\text{,}\) and \(f'''(x) = \sin(x)\text{.}\) Thus, evaluating these at \(x = \pi/3\text{,}\) we get the terms term 0 = \({1\over2}\) term 1 = \(-{1\sqrt3\over2}(x - {\pi\over3})\) term 2 = \(-{{1\over4}}(x - {\pi\over3})^2\text{,}\) and term 3 = \({{1\over4\sqrt3}}(x - {\pi\over3})^3\text{.}\) Thus the series is
\begin{equation*} \cos(x) = {1\over2} - {1\sqrt3\over2}(x - {\pi\over3}) - {{1\over4}}(x - {\pi\over3})^2 + {{1\over4\sqrt3}}(x - {\pi\over3})^3 + \cdots. \end{equation*}
