Solution 1.5.4.2.1.

\(W=f(c)\) where \(W\) is weight in pounds and \(c\) is the number of Calories consumed per day: (a)\(f(1500) = 150\) means that consuming 1500 cal each day results in a weight of 150 lb (the units of 1500 are cal, and 150 lb). (b)\(f'(2000)=0\) means that consuming 2000 cal each day causes neither weight gain nor loss (the units of the 2000 are cal, and 0 lb/cal). (c)\(f^{-1}(160)=2100\) means that a weight of 160 lb is caused by a consumption of 2100 cal each day (the units of 160 are lb, and 2100 cal). (d) The units of \(dW/dc\) are lb/cal (e) Based on Sam's conclusion, \(f'(2800)\) should equal \(\frac{\Delta W}{\Delta c} = \frac{0.4}{40} = 0.01\text{.}\)
