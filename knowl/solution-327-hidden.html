\(\int\,u\,v'\,dx = u\cdot v - \int u'\,v\,dx\)) with \(u = z+1\) and \(v' = e^{3 z}\text{,}\) so that \(u' = 1\) and \(v = {1\over 3} e^{3 z}\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} \int\,\left(z+1\right)e^{3z}\,dz = \frac{z+1}{3}\,e^{3 z} - \int\,{1\over 3} e^{3 z}\,dz = \frac{z+1}{3}\,e^{3 z} - {1\over 9} e^{3 z} + C. \end{equation*}
