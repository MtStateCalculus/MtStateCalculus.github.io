Solution 8.6.4.1.1.

\(\displaystyle \frac{4}{1-9 x} = 4 \cdot \sum_{n=0}^\infty (9 x)^n\text{.}\) Then we can see that \(c_n = 4 \cdot 9^n\text{,}\) making \(c_0 = 4\text{,}\) \(c_1 = 4 \cdot 9 = 36\text{,}\) \(c_2 = 4 \cdot 9^2 = 324\text{,}\) etc. Regarding the radius of convergence, we perform the standard ratio test:
\begin{equation*} \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| \frac{4(9 x)^{n+1}}{4(9 x)^n} \right| = \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| 9 x \right| = \left| 9 x \right| \end{equation*}
And then \(\left| 9 x \right| \lt 1 \iff \left| x \right| \lt \frac 1 {9}\text{,}\) making \(R = \frac 1 {9}\text{.}\)
