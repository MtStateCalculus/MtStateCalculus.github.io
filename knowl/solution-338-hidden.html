SOLUTION

Multiplying by the least common denominator gives

\begin{equation*} 3x^{2}+4x+47 = A(x^2+9)+(Bx+C)(x+5) \end{equation*}

Rearranging terms on the right hand side, yields

\begin{equation*} 3x^{2}+4x+47 = (A+B)x^2+(5B+C)x + 9 A+ 5C \end{equation*}

Now we equate the coefficients:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rl} A+B \amp = 3 \\ 5B +C \amp = 4 \\ 9 A + 5C \amp = 47 \end{array} \end{equation*}

Solving the system gives \(A=3, \; B = 0\) and \(C= 4\) so the partial fraction decomposition is

\begin{equation*} \frac{3x^{2}+4x+47}{(x+5)(x^2+9)} = \frac{3}{x+5} + \frac{4}{x^2+9} \end{equation*}

The definite integral is then

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \int \frac{3x^{2}+4x+47}{(x+5)(x^2+9)} dx = 3\ln\!\left(\left|x+5\right|\right) + \frac{4\tan^{-1}\!\left(\frac{x}{3}\right)}{3} +C \end{equation*}
