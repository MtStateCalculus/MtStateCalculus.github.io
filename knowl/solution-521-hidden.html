\(\displaystyle \frac{5}{x}\) and its first three derivatives are \(f(x) = \displaystyle \frac{5}{x}\text{,}\) \(f'(x) = -{5\over x^2}\text{,}\) \(f''(x) = {10\over x^3}\text{,}\) and \(f'''(x) = -{30\over x^4}\text{.}\) Thus, evaluating these at \(x = 4\text{,}\) we get the terms term 0 = \({{5\over4}}\) term 1 = \(-{{5\over16}}(x - 4)\) term 2 = \({{5\over64}}(x - 4)^2\text{,}\) and term 3 = \(-{{5\over256}}(x - 4)^3\text{.}\) Thus the series is
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \frac{5}{x} = {{5\over4}} - {{5\over16}}(x - 4) + {{5\over64}}(x - 4)^2 - {{5\over256}}(x - 4)^3 + \cdots. \end{equation*}
