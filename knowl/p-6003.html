Multiplying by the least common denominator gives

\begin{equation*} 4x^{2}-x+41 = A(x^2+9)+(Bx+C)(x - 5) \end{equation*}

Rearranging terms on the right hand side, yields

\begin{equation*} 4x^{2}-x+41 = (A+B)x^2+(-5B+C)x + 9 A - 5C \end{equation*}

Now we equate the coefficients:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rl} A+B \amp = 4 \\ -5B +C \amp = -1 \\ 9 A - 5C \amp = 41 \end{array} \end{equation*}

Solving the system gives \(A=4, \; B = 0\) and \(C= -1\) so the partial fraction decomposition is

\begin{equation*} \frac{4x^{2}-x+41}{(x - 5)(x^2+9)} = \frac{4}{x - 5} + \frac{-1}{x^2+9} \end{equation*}

The definite integral is then

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \int \frac{4x^{2}-x+41}{(x - 5)(x^2+9)} dx = 4\ln\!\left(\left|x-5\right|\right) + \frac{-1\tan^{-1}\!\left(\frac{x}{3}\right)}{3} +C \end{equation*}
