Exercise 1. Matching solutions with equations.

Match the solutions to the differential equations. If there is more than one solution to an equation, select the answer that includes all solutions.

  1. \(\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx}=3 y\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx}=-3 y\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=9 y\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=-9 y\)

  1. \(\displaystyle y = \sin(3 x)\)

  2. \(\displaystyle y = e^{3 x}\)

  3. \(\displaystyle y = 3\sin(x)\)

  4. \(\displaystyle y = e^{-3 x}\)

  5. \(y = e^{-3 x}\) or \(y = e^{3 x}\)

  6. \(y = \sin(3 x)\) or \(y = 3\sin(x)\)
