Solution 4.4.5.3.1.

Each part of this integral can be done separately, and each is a special case of the general rule \(\displaystyle \int u^n du = \frac{u^{n+1}}{n+1}\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int_{4}^{5} (2 x + 7) dx \amp= 2 \int_{4}^{5} x\, dx + 7 \int_{4}^{5} dx\\ \amp= 2 \left( \left. \frac{x^2}{2} \right|_{4}^{5} \right) + 7 \left( \left. x \right|_{4}^{5} \right)\\ \amp= 2 \left( \frac{5^2}{2} - \frac{4^2}{2} \right) + 7 \left( 5 - 4 \right) = {16} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
