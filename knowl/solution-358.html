Solution 6.1.5.2.1.

Over the interval \([0,\pi]\text{,}\) the graphs \(y = 6\sin x\) and \(y = 9 \cos x\) intersect when \(6\sin x = 9 \cos x\text{,}\) i.e., \(\tan x = \frac{9}{6}\text{,}\) i.e., \(x=\tan^{-1}(\frac{9}{6})=0.982793723247329\text{.}\) Let \(t = \tan^{-1}(\frac{9}{6})\text{.}\) Over the interval \(\left[0, t\right]\text{,}\) the graph of \(y = 6\sin x\) lies below that of \(y = 9 \cos x\text{.}\) Hence, the area between the two curves is \(\int_0^{ t}(9 \cos x-6 \sin x)dx\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} \amp =\left.(9\sin x+6\cos x)\right|_0^{ t} \cr \amp =\left(9\sin t+6\cos t\right)-(9\cdot 0+6\cdot 1)\cr \amp =\left(9\sin t+6\cos t\right)-6 \end{array} \end{equation*}
Since \(t = \tan^{-1}(\frac{9}{6})\text{,}\) we have \(\tan t = \frac{9}{6}\text{.}\) Considering a rightangle triangle with an angle which opposite side has length 9 and adjacent side has length 6, we see that the hypotenues is \(\sqrt{117}\text{.}\) Thus \(\sin t = \frac{9}{\sqrt{117}}\) and \(\cos t = \frac{6}{\sqrt{117}}\text{.}\) \(9\sin t+6\cos t = \frac{9^2}{\sqrt{117}} + \frac{6^2}{\sqrt{117}} = \frac{9^2+6^2}{\sqrt{117}} = \frac{117}{\sqrt{117}}=\sqrt{117}\text{.}\) Thus, \(\int_0^{ t}(9 \cos x-6 \sin x)dx= \sqrt{117}-6.\) Over the interval \(\left[ t,\pi\right]\text{,}\) the graph of \(y = 6\sin x\) lies above that of \(y = 9 \cos x\text{.}\) Hence, the area between the two curves is \(\int^\pi_{ t}(6\sin x - 9 \cos x)dx\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} \amp =-\left.(9\sin x+6\cos x)\right|^\pi_{ t} \cr \amp =6 + \sqrt{117} \end{array} \end{equation*}
Thus the total area is the sum of the above two integrals, which is \(2\sqrt{117}\text{.}\)
