Solution 3.4.3.2.1.

SOLUTION

Let \(w\) and \(h\) denote the width of the base and the height of the container, respectively.

Since the length of the base is twice the width, the volume of the container is

\begin{equation*} V= (2w)(w)h =2 w^2 h = 20 \end{equation*}

so

\begin{equation*} h = \frac{20}{ 2 w^2} \end{equation*}

The sides have total area \(2(2w)h+ 2wh = 6wh\text{,}\) while the base has area \(w(2w) = 2 w^2\text{,}\) so the cost is

\begin{equation*} 4 \cdot (6wh ) + 15\cdot (2 w^2) \end{equation*}

Substuting the expression for \(h\) gives

\begin{equation*} C(w) = 4 \cdot 6 w\cdot \frac{20}{ 2 w^2} + 15\cdot( 2 w^2 ) = \frac{240}{w} + 30 w^2 \quad w >0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} C'(w) = -\frac{240}{w^2} + 60 w = \frac{-240+ 60 w^3}{w^2} \end{equation*}

\(\displaystyle w = \left(4\right)^{1/3}\) is the critical number. There is an absolute minimum at this critical number since \(C'(w) \lt 0\) for \(0 \lt w \lt \left(4\right)^{1/3}\) and \(C'(w) >0\) for \(w > \left(4\right)^{1/3}\text{.}\)

The total minimum cost is

\begin{equation*} C\left(\left(4\right)^{1/3} \right) \approx \$226.79 \end{equation*}
