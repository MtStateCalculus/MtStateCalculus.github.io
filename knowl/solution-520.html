Solution 8.5.6.3.1.

\(\displaystyle \frac{5}{x}\) and its first three derivatives are \(f(x) = \displaystyle \frac{5}{x}\text{,}\) \(f'(x) = -{5\over x^2}\text{,}\) \(f''(x) = {10\over x^3}\text{,}\) and \(f'''(x) = -{30\over x^4}\text{.}\) Thus, evaluating these at \(x = -1\text{,}\) we get the terms term 0 = \({-5}\) term 1 = \(-{5}(x + 1)\) term 2 = \({-5}(x + 1)^2\text{,}\) and term 3 = \(-{5}(x + 1)^3\text{.}\) Thus the series is
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \frac{5}{x} = {-5} - {5}(x + 1) + {-5}(x + 1)^2 - {5}(x + 1)^3 + \cdots. \end{equation*}
