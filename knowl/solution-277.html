Solution 4.4.5.3.1.

Each part of this integral can be done separately, and each is a special case of the general rule \(\displaystyle \int u^n du = \frac{u^{n+1}}{n+1}\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int_{2}^{7} (4 x + 6) dx \amp= 4 \int_{2}^{7} x\, dx + 6 \int_{2}^{7} dx\\ \amp= 4 \left( \left. \frac{x^2}{2} \right|_{2}^{7} \right) + 6 \left( \left. x \right|_{2}^{7} \right)\\ \amp= 4 \left( \frac{7^2}{2} - \frac{2^2}{2} \right) + 6 \left( 7 - 2 \right) = {120} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
