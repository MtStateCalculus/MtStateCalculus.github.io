Solution 2.7.3.5.1.

Differentiating implicitly with respect to \(x\) gives

\begin{equation*} 4 y^3 + 12 x y^2 \, \frac{dy}{dx} + y + x \, \frac{dy}{dx} = 0. \end{equation*}

If \(x = 7\) and \(y = 1\text{,}\) then this equation becomes

\begin{equation*} 5 + 91 \, \frac{dy}{dx} = 0, \qquad \text{and so} \qquad \frac{dy}{dx} = -{\frac{5}{91}}. \end{equation*}

If \((x, y)\) is any point on the tangent line besides \(\left(7,1\right)\) then

\begin{equation*} -{\frac{5}{91}} = \frac{y-1}{x-7}, \qquad \text{or} \qquad 5\!\left(x-7\right) = -91\!\left(y-1\right). \end{equation*}

Therefore, \(5x+91y = 126\) is an equation of the line tangent to the curve at the point \(\left(7,1\right)\text{.}\)
